// Skeleton Anatomy — All 8 Zones
```
🦴
Spine
30mm OD CF tube — central load bearer
  • Single vertical CF tube, floor to neck base
  • All other zones bolt to this via aluminum collars
  • ~100cm total length at life-size
  • Mounted in weighted base plate for stability
🫁
Ribcage Frame
CF flat sheet + 20mm tubes — chest structure
  • Two CF sheet "ribs" front and back
  • Connected by horizontal CF cross-tubes
  • Servo mounts 3D printed in PETG, epoxied to CF
  • Electronics bay recessed inside
🔩
Shoulder Assembly
Aluminum pivot block + CF arm rail
  • CNC aluminum shoulder joint — rotates on bearing
  • DS3225 servo drives the joint (25kg torque)
  • CF tube slides into aluminum socket, epoxied
  • Left/right symmetric — design once, mirror it
💪
Arms (Upper + Lower)
20mm CF tubes — telescoping sections
  • Upper arm: 20mm CF outer tube
  • Lower arm: 16mm CF inner tube slides inside
  • Spring-loaded extension or servo-driven
  • Elbow joint: aluminum pivot + small bearing
🦷
Neck + Head Mount
16mm CF tube + pan servo bracket
  • Short CF tube from spine top to head base
  • Aluminum pan bracket for ±45° head rotation
  • Head weight: ~2kg — neck CF must be rigid
  • All wiring routed through hollow CF tube
🦋
Pelvis / Hip
CF flat sheet + aluminum brackets
  • Wide CF sheet as hip platform
  • Battery mounts HERE — keeps center of gravity low
  • Bolts to spine via central aluminum collar
  • Leg attachment points on each side
🦵
Legs (Static)
25mm CF tubes — structural support only
  • No movement — purely structural at this stage
  • Wide stance base for stability
  • CF tubes bolted into weighted foot platforms
  • Foot platform: aluminum plate + rubber grip
✋
Hands / Wrists
3D printed PETG + small CF inserts
  • Wrist pivot: small aluminum bracket
  • Hand shell: 3D printed in PETG, not structural
  • CF rod runs through palm for rigidity
  • Fingers: foam over wire armature
```

// Week-by-Week Plan
01
Order + CAD
Order all CF tubes and sheets from RockWest Composites
Order aluminum collars and shoulder blocks from McMaster-Carr
Model full skeleton in Fusion 360 — every joint, every length
Print test joints in PLA first to verify fit before PETG
02
Spine + Pelvis
Cut spine tube to exact length (diamond disc + respirator)
Attach aluminum collars at each zone point
Cut and attach hip CF sheet to pelvis collar
Mount foot plates — test freestanding stability
03
Chest + Shoulders
Cut chest rib sheets — sand edges smooth
Epoxy rib sheets to spine collar cross-tubes
Mount aluminum shoulder pivot blocks
Test DS3225 servo in shoulder — check torque under arm weight
04
Arms + Head + Finish
Assemble telescoping arm sections
Mount neck tube + pan bracket
Print and attach all PETG joint housings
Full skeleton standing test — load check, wiggle test
⚠ Safety — Non-Negotiable
😷Respirator P100 — CF dust causes permanent lung damage. Every single cut.
🥽Safety glasses — CF splinters are invisible and sharp. Always.
🧤Nitrile gloves — CF fibers embed in skin. Epoxy causes sensitization.
🌬️Work outdoors or with ventilation fan — never cut CF indoors without airflow.
💧Wet-cut when possible — water keeps CF dust from becoming airborne.
🗑️Bag all CF waste immediately — seal it before removing your respirator.

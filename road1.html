// Day-by-Day Plan
01
Install + Explore
Download and install Fusion 360 (free personal license). Watch the UI overview video. Click every menu. Don't build anything yet — just get comfortable navigating.
Learn
02
Sketching Basics
Watch Kevin Kennedy "Fusion 360 for Beginners" Lesson 1–2. Learn: Line, Rectangle, Circle, Dimension, Constraint. Draw a 2D profile of Baymax's torso outline — just the shape, no depth.
Learn
03
Extrusion + Bodies
Lesson 3–4. Learn Extrude, Revolve, Fillet. Take your Day 2 torso sketch — extrude it into a 3D block. Add fillets to round the edges. Export as STL and look at it in a slicer.
Design
04
Order Day
Order everything on the hardware list: Bambu A1 Mini, Arduino Mega starter kit, DS3225 servo pack (×6), PCA9685 servo driver, 12V LiPo battery + charger. Budget ~$600 today.
Order
05
Model a Tube
Model a hollow cylinder in Fusion 360 matching your CF spine tube dimensions (30mm OD, 27mm ID, 100mm long). Practice Shell command. This is your first real Baymax part.
Design
06
Model a Joint
Model a simple pivot bracket — a U-shaped aluminum clamp that would hold a CF tube. Add bolt holes. This is the logic of every joint in the skeleton. Learn: Hole tool, Mirror, Pattern.
Design
07
Rest + Review
Look at your concept art sketches carefully. Measure proportions with a ruler on the page. Write down: torso height, arm length, head diameter, leg length — all as ratios to total height. These become your CAD dimensions.
Learn
08
Printer Arrives
Unbox Bambu A1 Mini. Follow setup guide exactly. Run the auto-calibration. Print the included test model. Don't skip calibration — a poorly calibrated printer wastes every part you make in Month 2.
Build
09
Tolerance Test
Print a tolerance test: two cylinders designed to fit together. Print at 0.1mm, 0.2mm, 0.3mm clearance. Find which fits best. Your skeleton joints need this number — don't guess it.
Test
10
Assembly in Fusion
Learn Fusion 360 Assembly workspace. Joints, constraints, degrees of freedom. Assemble your tube + bracket from Days 5–6 into a moving hinge. Verify it rotates correctly in simulation.
Design
11
Print Bracket v1
Print the joint bracket you designed on Day 6. Test fit with a pen or dowel the same diameter as your CF tube. Almost certainly won't fit perfectly — adjust tolerance by +0.2mm and reprint.
Test
12
Shoulder Joint CAD
Design the Baymax shoulder joint specifically. This is the most complex joint — it needs to hold a DS3225 servo AND a CF arm tube AND rotate smoothly. Spend the full day on this. It's worth it.
Design
13
Print Shoulder v1
Print the shoulder joint. Test with servo. Test with tube. Document every problem. The goal isn't a perfect part — the goal is a list of what needs changing. Expect 2–3 iterations this month.
Test
14
Revise + Reprint
Fix every problem from Day 13. Reprint shoulder joint. Test again. This cycle — design, print, test, fix — is the entire skill of hardware engineering. You're learning it right now.
Build
15
Arduino Hello World
Install Arduino IDE. Connect Arduino Mega via USB. Open the Blink example sketch. Upload it. Watch the LED blink. This is your first embedded program. Understand every line of that 10-line sketch.
Learn
16
Servo Basics
Wire one MG996R servo to Arduino (signal pin 9, 5V, GND). Load Servo library example "Sweep". Watch it move. Change the delay. Change the degrees. Understand what each number does.
Learn
17
Multiple Servos
Wire PCA9685 board to Arduino via I2C. Install Adafruit PCA9685 library. Control 3 servos simultaneously from one board. This is the system that will run all of Baymax's joints.
Build
18
Arm Sequence
Program a "Baymax arm raise" sequence: both shoulders move from 0° to 90° simultaneously, pause 1 second, return. Use arrays to store positions. Add easing (slow start, slow end) for natural movement.
Build
19
Serial Monitor
Learn to use Serial Monitor for debugging. Print servo positions as they move. Send commands from keyboard to control servos ("L" = left arm up, "R" = right arm up). This is your first remote control.
Learn
20
LED Eyes Preview
Wire a NeoPixel ring (or strip) to Arduino. Install FastLED library. Make it glow Baymax eye color (soft warm white). Program a slow breathing pulse. This is a preview of Month 4's eye system.
Build
21
Electronics Plan
Draw the complete wiring diagram for the final Baymax on paper. Every servo labeled, every sensor, Arduino Mega, PCA9685, Raspberry Pi, speaker, battery. This is your electrical blueprint for Month 3.
Design
22
Full Skeleton CAD
Start the full skeleton assembly in Fusion 360. Begin with spine — a single 30mm CF tube, 100cm. Add hip collar and pelvis platform. Every dimension must match your Day 7 proportions exactly.
Design
23
Chest + Shoulders CAD
Add ribcage frame to skeleton assembly. Position shoulder pivot blocks at correct height. Verify left-right symmetry using Mirror in Fusion 360. Add arm tubes — both upper and lower sections.
Design
24
Legs + Head CAD
Add leg tubes to hip platform. Add neck tube to spine top. Add a placeholder head sphere. Now step back — does it look like Baymax? Compare to your reference sketches and adjust proportions.
Design
25
Measure + BOM
Use Fusion 360 to measure every CF tube length in your assembly. Export a Bill of Materials — every part, every dimension, every quantity. This becomes your Month 2 shopping list for RockWest Composites.
Design
26
Joint Details
Go back into every joint in the assembly and add detail — bolt hole positions, collar depths, servo pocket dimensions. The skeleton CAD needs to be buildable, not just visual. Every joint must make physical sense.
Design
27
Order CF Materials
Place the RockWest Composites order using your BOM from Day 25. Order McMaster-Carr aluminum collars and brackets. Allow 1 week shipping. Everything needs to arrive before Day 35.
Order
28–30
Buffer + Polish
Three days to fix everything you're behind on. Reprint shoulder joints. Finish wiring diagram. Finalize CAD. Watch ahead on Month 2 CF cutting techniques. If you're fully caught up — start printing PETG joint housings early.
Build
// Tools + Software You Need
🧊
Fusion 360
Free for personal use. Industry-standard CAD and CAM. Everything you design for Baymax lives here. Download the personal license — it has everything you need.
autodesk.com/fusion360
🔵
Arduino IDE 2
Free. Write, compile, and upload code to your Arduino Mega. Version 2 has autocomplete and a much better debugger than version 1. Essential from Day 15.
arduino.cc/software
🐼
Bambu Studio
Free slicer for your Bambu printer. Converts Fusion 360 STL exports into printer instructions. Comes pre-configured for A1 Mini — barely needs setup.
bambulab.com/software
📐
Digital Calipers
Buy a 150mm digital caliper (~$15 on Amazon). You will use this every single day. Measure printed parts, CF tubes, servo housings. The difference between 19.8mm and 20mm matters.
amazon — "digital caliper 150mm"
▶️
Product Design Online
YouTube channel by Kevin Kennedy. Best Fusion 360 beginner series available. Free. Watch in order, do every exercise. 6 hours total — spread over Weeks 1–2.
YouTube — "Product Design Online"
⚡
Paul McWhorter Arduino
YouTube series "Arduino for Beginners" — 70+ videos, incredibly patient and thorough. Start at Lesson 1. By Week 3 you'll be at Lesson 10–15. Free.
YouTube — "Paul McWhorter Arduino"
⚡ Month 1 Rules
01
Never skip the test prints. A joint that looks right in CAD fails in real life constantly. Print, test, fix. That loop is the job.
02
Don't buy random parts. Every purchase this month is on the list. Off-list buying wastes money and creates clutter that slows you down in Month 2.
03
The CAD skeleton on Day 25 doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be complete enough to generate real dimensions. You will revise it in Month 2.
04
If you fall behind — skip Day 7, 14, or 21 rest days. They're buffers, not mandatory breaks. The hard deadline is placing the CF order on Day 27.
